ANATOMY OF TEMAKI

CUCUMBER

Cucumber is what I consider a "cool" food - this means that when my body is heating up or when it's really hot outside, cucumber is a great way to resolve that heat. Other cool foods include tomatoes, watermelon, crabs, soft cheeses, seaweed, peppermint etc.

There are also hot foods, that have a reverse effect and include egg yolk, ginger, garlic, coffee, lychee, and deep fried foods.

To be very honest, I never knew the reasoning behind the classification, just that I knew which foods fell into which category and it became intuition on when I would eat them.

CRAB

While in sushi it is usually imitation crab, my favourite type of crab is "Chinese Mitten Crab", or 大閘蟹. The best time to eat it is from October to December but after coming to college, I haven't had a chance to eat any :(

I actually still don't know what is in imitation crab, only that I really like eating it. It's kind of like those foods like fish cake or spam where you don't really know what it is but it's still very much part of the foods you eat regularly.

TAMAGO

It's on my Amazon wishlist but there's a special type of pan for making Tamagoyaki, which is the type of egg you find on sushi.

It's amazing the number of ways that you could eat or make egg and I want to be able to try as many variations of the egg dish as possible. Most of what I've had are ones that I can make at home or ones in Chinese or Japanese cuisine.

I think even with the specific ways (i.e. scrambled eggs) there is still so much variation. For example I like making mine with a little bit of milk and, let it be a bit runny but my friend only likes it with pure eggs.

IKURA

I've never had popping boba before coming to America but it kinda reminds me of ikura.

I think that it's the texture that makes this such a polarising food because you bite into it and it just bursts in your mouth and if you're not a fan of the "fishy" flavour, you're probably not going to have a good time.
